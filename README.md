## üìå Project Description  
This project involves the design and development of an **interactive Excel-based sales dashboard** for **Kmart**. The dashboard consolidates raw sales data into a visually appealing and easy-to-use interface, providing **actionable insights for business decision-making**.  

The goal was to **transform raw Kmart sales data into an interactive and insightful report**, enabling stakeholders to analyze performance trends, identify top-performing products, and make informed decisions.

## üìå Deep Dive Analysis

**Sales Trend Analysis**

- **Quarterly average sales peaked between Q2 and Q3 in 2023, with a similar pattern in 2024 at Q3**. However, both years show a similar dip during Q4. This highlights stronger customer purchasing during Q2-Q3, likely driven by the Christmas season, followed by easing demand in Q4.

- The **significant Q4 decline** was observed primarily in **Kmart Online sales** suggests that customers continue to favor in-store shopping during the holiday period.

**Recommendations:**
- Given that average sales peak between Q2 and Q3, **leverage this period with targeted promotions, product launches, and marketing campaigns** to maximize revenue during the high-demand season.
- The sharp dip in Kmart Online sales in Q4 indicates a preference for in-store shopping during the holiday period. Consider strategies such as:
    - **Enhancing online holiday promotions and incentives** (e.g., limited-time discounts, free shipping).
    - **Implementing in-store pickup options** to bridge online and offline experiences.
    - **Improving online merchandising and holiday campaign visibility** to compete with in-store traffic.
- **Strengthen the integration between online and physical stores**, ensuring a seamless customer journey. Personalised communications and promotions based on shopping behavior (online vs. in-store) can help retain and grow sales across both channels.
- **Use historical sales trends** to optimize inventory allocation, ensuring sufficient stock in-store during Q4 while balancing online inventory **to reduce lost sales opportunities**.
- **Continuously track shifts in online vs. in-store behavior** during peak seasons. This will enable proactive adjustments in marketing, promotions, and operational planning to capture more holiday sales online.

**Kmart Online notable Q4 dips**
- The notable Q4 dip, particularly in Kmart Online, was primarily driven by 3 categories.
    - In 2023, **Toys & Outdoor Play** experienced a sharp Q4 decline, but sales picked up again in 2024.
    - **Clothing & Fashion** experienced a sharp Q4 decline in both 2023 and 2024, with the drop being particularly pronounced in 2024.
    - **Beauty & Personal Care** showed a sharp Q4 decline in both 2023 and 2024, with the 2024 decline starting notably earlier, spanning Q2 to Q4.

- **Implement a stronger online holiday campaign and early Q4 promotions** to counter the pronounced decline in 2024.
- **Introduce mid-year campaigns and loyalty incentive**s to sustain throughout the year.
- **Align inventory and marketing efforts** with category specific trends to prevent stock out or overstocks, ensuring optimal availability during peak periods and targeted promotions.
